# AI Governance Constitutional Control Plane — Core Architecture Diagram

```
AUTHORITY PLANE
  BIG G (Human Authority)
        ↓
  Governance Doctrine
        ↓
CONTROL PLANE
  PMS Governance OS
        ↓
  Runtime Authority Manager (RAM)
        ↓
EXECUTION PLANE
  QGED (Execution Gate)
        ↓
  ALCATARA (Decision Engine)
        ↓
  Operational Systems (Finance, Logistics, APIs)
        ↓
EVIDENCE SYSTEM
  Immutable Ledger / Audit / Trace
```

---

**Key Principles:**
- **Authority Plane:** Defines legitimacy and origin of authority (BIG G, doctrine, topology).
- **Control Plane:** Interprets authority, enforces policy, and validates runtime decisions (PMS, RAM as governance kernel).
- **Execution Plane:** Executes validated actions (QGED, ALCATARA, operational systems).
- **Evidence System:** Cross-plane, records all actions for audit and compliance.

**Deep Principle:**
> Intelligence may generate decisions, but authority determines whether those decisions can become reality.

**Summary:**
This architecture merges distributed systems control-plane logic with constitutional governance, making governance executable and operational — not just policy. RAM (Runtime Authority Manager) is the governance kernel enforcing authority at runtime.
