Regulatory Architecture Model (RAM)
What, Why, When, How, and Where — BPB Solutions LTD
What is RAM?
RAM (Regulatory Architecture Model) is BPB Solutions’ structured framework for governance, compliance, and operational discipline. It integrates technical controls (compliance automation, audit protocols, authority logic) and operational processes (review cycles, onboarding, risk oversight) to ensure capital integrity and regulatory alignment across all business lines.
Why RAM?
RAM is designed to elevate BPB Solutions from standard IT service to institutional-grade governance operator. It provides:
  • Regulatory defensibility and auditability
  • Systematic provenance and artifact generation
  • Capital discipline and risk management
  • Structured transparency for board, regulatory, and investor review
When is RAM Used?
RAM is applied throughout BPB’s operational lifecycle:
  • During project initiation and onboarding
  • For every governance artifact, report, and dashboard
  • In quarterly and monthly review cycles
  • When preparing for audits, regulatory filings, or capital events
How Does RAM Work?
Technically, RAM is implemented via the AA Workspace Governance Framework (see linked PDF), which details:
  • Authority logic and compliance automation
  • Audit documentation and release validation protocols
  • Immutable ledger and artifact versioning
Operationally, RAM is embedded in dashboards, transparency reports, and risk/oversight frameworks, ensuring continuous discipline and review.
